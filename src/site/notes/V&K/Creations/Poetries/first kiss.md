the elevator—
half light, half fear,
half of forever squeezed into thirty seconds.

it wasn’t cinematic.
no violins, no sunsets,
no perfect place.
just the quiet hum of the lift
and two trembling bodies
trying to memorize each other
before the world arrived again.

the boy who hid behind silence
and insecurity, and inferiority,
now holding her face, pulling towards his,
like the most fragile revolution.

when her lips met mine,
everything i hated about myself
went still.
every doubt lost its vocabulary.

the elevator moved,
but time didn’t.
if only i could freeze a moment,
i would freeze this,
forever.

the touch of her lips,
the touch of her hands on my body,
her breath on my face,
the smell of her body,
and the warmth that travelled from her pulse to mine
—all were my haven and sanctuary.

i was devastated before coming here.
the loss, the guilt,
the things i didn’t get to say—
everything was overwhelming.
and somehow,
today, she made me hear
“you’re forgiven”
without ever saying it.

because she showed up
when her body screamed for rest.
because she still smiled,
when her cramps would’ve made anyone curse.
because love,
in her language,
means showing up anyway.
and she loves me to the moon
and back.

and... i,
a mere being,
doubted the love of my goddess?
multiple times?
how diabolical!

but from now onwards,
i won't.
never ever.
because we are married,
for this lifetime and beyond.
no vows, no rings,
just exchanged breaths in an elevator—
and somewhere between them,
our souls signed the paper,
on a sacred date.

the number twenty-one.
the date the universe kissed me twice.
once, when she was born.
and again, when she kissed me.
twenty-one:
the number that keeps me breathing,
the reason i rise before alarms,
the fire behind my muscles,
the calm behind my voice.
everything i’ve built
the confidence, the art, the body, the courage
was born from her.
she’s not just my muse;
she’s my milestone.
and every 21st of anything
feels like the world whispering,
“live again.”

and now, when i say forever,
it’s not a promise.
it’s evidence.
of the boy reborn in her arms,
of lips that stitched the cracks he hid,
of two hearts that burned their hate for romance,
just for each other,
and called it life.
